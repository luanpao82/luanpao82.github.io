Seongho An

I study how nonprofits use digital technology for advocacy, how organizations collaborate across sectors, and how computational methods can deepen our understanding of civil society. My work is driven by a simple conviction: rigorous research should be useful to practitioners, policymakers, and the communities that nonprofits serve.

Current Projects

Text Analysis · Asia

Asian Nonprofit Research Database

A text analysis of 16,158 articles from eight Asian locations (1984–2026), mapping publication patterns and thematic trends in nonprofit scholarship across the region.

Explore Database →

Longitudinal Data · ARNOVA

ARNOVA Conference History Database

A longitudinal dataset of 5,591 presentations from 2,011 institutions across 14 years of ARNOVA annual conferences (2012–2025), enabling trend analysis of nonprofit scholarship.

Explore Database →

Policy Analysis · Immigration

State Immigration Policy & Nonprofit Financial Capacity

Examines how state-level immigration policies affect the financial health of 2,300+ immigration-serving nonprofits across 51 U.S. states (2000–2020), using two-way fixed effects modeling.

View Project →

Community Engagement · Prevention

Human Trafficking Prevention Education for Immigrants

A community-academic partnership delivering human trafficking prevention education to ESL populations in Central Florida, funded by UCF Downtown Community Engaged Scholarship.
